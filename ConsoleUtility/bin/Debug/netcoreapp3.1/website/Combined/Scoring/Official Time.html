9.10 Official Time

9.10.1 (Action Air only)
Only the timing device operated by a Range Officer and/or the Stop Plate included in the COF must be used to record the official elapsed time of a competitor's attempt at a course of fire. If a Range Officer assigned to a course of fire (or a more senior Match Official) deems that a timing device or Stop Plate is faulty, a competitor whose attempt cannot be credited with an accurate time will be required to reshoot the course of fire.
9.10.2
If, in the opinion of an Arbitration Committee, the time credited to a competitor for a course of fire is deemed to be unrealistic, the competitor will be required to reshoot the course of fire (see Rule 9.7.4).
9.10.3 (Action Air only)
When a Stop Plate is used in a COF, the competitor must shoot and hit the Stop Plate with his final shot, failing which the competitor will receive a zero score for the subject stage.
9.10.1 (Handgun | Shotgun | Rifle | Mini-Rifle | PCC only)
Only the timing device operated by a Range Officer must be used to record the official elapsed time of a competitor's attempt at a course of fire. If a Range Officer assigned to a course of fire (or a more senior Match Official) deems that a timing device is faulty, a competitor whose attempt cannot be credited with an accurate time will be required to reshoot the course of fire.
